## Privacy policy

Effective date: 22 July 2026

Catch is operated by **[CONFIRM: exact legal operator name]** ("Catch", "we",

catchdates.com, a Catch mobile application, an organizer or event page, a host
tool, or contact our support team.

### Data we collect

- Information you provide, such as your name, email address, phone number,
  date of birth or age confirmation, city, account credentials, profile details,
  photos, prompts, dating or activity preferences, organizer information, event
  information, reviews, messages, support requests, reports, and claim requests.
- Event and interaction information, such as registrations, waitlists,
  attendance and check-in, private catches, mutual matches, blocks, reports,
  host actions, saved organizers, and aggregate event outcomes.
- Website lead information, including name, email, city, role, organization,
  public website or social link, host application answers, and attribution data
  attached to the form submission.
- Device and technical information, such as app version, operating system,
  browser, device identifiers, IP-derived security signals, diagnostic events,
  crash information, and approximate location. We use precise location only
  where a feature needs it and your device permission allows it.
- Public-source listing information. We may collect factual information about
  organizers and events from public websites and public social profiles,
  including names, descriptions, locations, dates, public links, and source
  provenance. A listing can be unclaimed; an unclaimed listing is not an
  endorsement or proof of a commercial relationship.

Please do not send sensitive information in free-text fields unless the feature
clearly requires it. Do not put payment credentials, government identifiers, or
another person's confidential information in a profile, review, or support
message.

### Why we use data

We use personal data to provide and secure Catch; create and manage accounts;
show relevant organizers and events; support registration, attendance, matching,
messaging, and safety features; operate organizer claims and host tools; answer
support, correction, takedown, and privacy requests; prevent fraud and abuse;
moderate content; understand product reliability and usage; communicate about
the service; comply with law; and establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

Where consent is required, you can withdraw it through the relevant setting or
by contacting us. Withdrawing consent does not make earlier lawful processing
invalid, but it may prevent us from providing a feature that needs that data.

### Public and private information

Organizer and event pages, reviews, organizer responses, and some profile facts
are public when the product labels them public. Public content may be indexed by
search engines. Private catches are not shown to the other person unless the
interest becomes mutual. Hosts receive operational and aggregate information;
they should not receive private dating choices unless a feature clearly says so.

### Sharing

We may share data with service providers that help us run hosting, databases,
authentication, storage, analytics, crash reporting, communications,
moderation, maps, search, and security. We may share information with an event
organizer when you ask to attend or use organizer-operated services. External
event and ticket links take you to a third party whose own privacy policy
applies. We may disclose information when required by law, to protect users or
the public, during a business reorganization, or with your direction. We do not
sell personal data for money.

Non-essential website analytics and marketing tags load only after you accept
them. Essential storage may be used for security, site operation, consent, saved
organizers, and form continuity.

### Retention and security

We keep data only as long as reasonably needed for the purposes above, legal
requirements, safety, dispute handling, fraud prevention, and backup recovery.
Retention differs by record. Public-source listings may remain while they are
accurate and relevant; source freshness is reviewed and correction or takedown
requests are available. Deleting an account starts the product's deletion
workflow, but limited records may remain where law, safety, fraud prevention,
or dispute resolution requires it.

We use access controls, encryption in transit, environment separation, service
authentication, audit logs, and monitoring intended to protect data. No system
is perfectly secure. Contact us promptly if you believe your account or data has
been compromised.

### Your choices and rights

Subject to applicable law, you may ask what personal data we process, request a
copy or summary, correct inaccurate or incomplete data, request erasure, withdraw
consent, nominate another person where the law provides, or raise a grievance.
You can also ask us to correct, claim, or remove an organizer or event listing.
We may need enough information to verify your identity and locate the relevant
record. We aim to answer privacy grievances within 90 days and consumer-service
complaints within one month where those timelines apply.

Send requests to **[CONFIRM: public privacy/support email]**. If you are not
satisfied with our response, you may use remedies available under applicable
law after first giving us a reasonable opportunity to address the grievance.

### Age requirement

Catch is for adults aged 18 or older. We do not knowingly offer dating services
to children. If you believe a person under 18 has provided data, contact us so
we can investigate and take appropriate action.

### International processing and changes

Our providers may process data outside your state or country, subject to their
contracts and applicable transfer restrictions. We may update this policy as
the service or law changes. We will post the updated date and provide additional
notice when a material change requires it.

## Help and safety

Catch support handles account access, privacy requests, listing claims and
corrections, event-page issues, safety reports, and website problems.

### Get help

Email **[CONFIRM: public support/privacy email]**. Include the page URL, your
account email or phone number if relevant, what happened, and the outcome you
need. Do not email passwords, one-time codes, full payment credentials,
government identifiers, or unnecessary sensitive details. We will acknowledge
and route the request; privacy grievances are targeted within 90 days and
consumer-service complaints within one month where applicable.

### Immediate safety

If someone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first. Catch
support is not an emergency service. Preserve relevant messages, event details,
receipts, and screenshots; use in-product block and report controls when
available; and tell us the event, organizer, account, and approximate time.

### Claim, correct, or remove a listing

Open the organizer page and use its claim action, or email support with the
listing URL. For an ownership claim, provide your role and a public organization
email, website update, social-account message, or another reasonable authority
signal. For a correction or takedown, identify the exact inaccurate, outdated,
private, infringing, unsafe, or unlawful content and explain the requested
change. We may ask for verification, preserve a decision receipt, and show a
claim or source-freshness state while reviewing it.

### Account and privacy requests

Use the app's account settings where available or email support to access,
correct, or delete your data, withdraw consent, or raise a grievance. Include
the account identifier needed to locate the record. We may verify identity
before disclosing or deleting data. Account deletion can remove product data
while limited safety, fraud, legal, dispute, and backup records remain for their
necessary retention period.

### Event and registration problems

Check whether the page labels the event as a Catch event or an external event.
For an external event, contact the organizer or ticketing provider for entry,
schedule, cancellation, payment, or refund issues. Send Catch the page URL if a
date, venue, registration link, price, or organizer fact appears wrong. Paid
events are not part of the current Catch website pilot.

### Website troubleshooting

Refresh the page, check your connection, disable strict script blocking for the
single action you are trying to complete, and retry in a current browser. If a
form fails, include the route, approximate time, browser/device, and visible
error. Do not send secrets. Non-essential analytics remain optional; declining
them should not prevent essential site or support access.
